Comets

Comets are icy celestial objects that release gas and dust when they approach the Sun, forming a glowing tail visible from Earth.

  • Comets are made primarily of ice, dust, and rocky material.
  • They originate in the outer parts of the solar system, such as the Kuiper Belt and Oort Cloud.
  • When near the Sun, the ice in comets vaporizes, creating a glowing coma and tail.
  • The tail of a comet always points away from the Sun due to solar wind.
  • Comets have highly elliptical orbits, bringing them close to the Sun and then far out again.
  • Famous comets include Halley's Comet, which is visible from Earth approximately every 76 years.
  • Comet nuclei are typically only a few kilometers wide.
  • Their bright appearances in the night sky have been recorded by many ancient civilizations.
  • Scientists study comets to learn about the early solar system since they are made of primordial material.
  • Space missions like ESA's Rosetta have provided valuable close-up data about comets.
