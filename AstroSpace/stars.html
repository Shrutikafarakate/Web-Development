Stars

Stars are massive luminous spheres of plasma held together by gravity. They emit light and heat, and our Sun is the closest star to Earth.

  • Stars are primarily composed of hydrogen and helium.
  • They form in nebulae, which are vast clouds of gas and dust.
  • The color of a star indicates its temperature; blue stars are the hottest, while red stars are cooler.
  • Stars undergo a life cycle, evolving from protostars to main sequence stars, and eventually to red giants or supernovae.
  • Our Sun is a middle-aged star, approximately 4.6 billion years old.
  • There are more stars in the universe than grains of sand on all the Earth's beaches.
  • Many stars exist in binary or multiple star systems, orbiting each other due to gravitational attraction.
  • Some stars, like Betelgeuse, are supergiants and are among the largest known stars.
  • When massive stars explode in supernovae, they can outshine entire galaxies for a short period.
  • Neutron stars, the remnants of supernovae, are incredibly dense and have strong magnetic fields.
