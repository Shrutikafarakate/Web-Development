Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest planet in our Solar System. It experiences extreme temperatures due to its very thin atmosphere.

  • Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System.
  • It has no moons or rings.
  • Mercury's surface temperatures range from -173°C at night to 427°C during the day.
  • A year on Mercury lasts only 88 Earth days.
  • It has a large metallic core, making it the second densest planet after Earth.
  • Mercury's surface is covered with craters, similar to Earth's Moon.
  • The planet has a very thin atmosphere composed mainly of oxygen, sodium, and hydrogen.
  • Mercury exhibits a phenomenon called "spin-orbit resonance," rotating three times for every two orbits around the Sun.
  • Despite being closest to the Sun, Mercury is not the hottest planet; Venus holds that title.
  • NASA's MESSENGER mission provided detailed information about Mercury's composition and magnetic field.
