Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. It rotates on its side, leading to extreme seasonal variations. Its blue-green hue is due to methane in its atmosphere.

  • Uranus was discovered by Sir William Herschel in 1781, making it the first planet discovered with a telescope. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}
  • The planet's name comes from the Greek god of the sky, Uranus, who was the grandfather of Zeus. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
  • Uranus has 27 known moons, including Titania, Oberon, Miranda, Ariel, and Umbriel. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • It has 13 known rings, composed mainly of dark particles. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
  • Uranus's atmosphere contains hydrogen, helium, and methane, giving it a blue-green color. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
  • The planet's magnetic field is unique; it is tilted and offset from the center of the planet. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
  • Uranus has the coldest atmosphere of any planet in the Solar System, with temperatures dropping to -224°C (-371°F). :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
  • It takes Uranus about 17 hours and 14 minutes to complete one rotation on its axis. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
  • One year on Uranus equals 84 Earth years, as it takes that long to orbit the Sun. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
  • Uranus was the first planet discovered in modern times, as it was not known to ancient civilizations. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
