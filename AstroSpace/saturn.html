Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in our solar system. Known for its stunning ring system, Saturn is a gas giant composed primarily of hydrogen and helium.

  • Saturn's rings are made mostly of ice particles, with traces of rock and dust.
  • It has 82 known moons, with Titan being the largest and more massive than Mercury.
  • Saturn's atmosphere is composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, with traces of methane and ammonia.
  • The planet's magnetic field is about 578 times stronger than Earth's.
  • Saturn's equatorial diameter is about 120,536 km, making it over nine times wider than Earth.
  • It takes Saturn approximately 10.7 hours to complete one rotation on its axis.
  • Saturn's average distance from the Sun is about 1.4 billion kilometers (9.5 AU).
  • The planet has a low density; it would float in water if there were a body of water large enough to contain it.
