Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life. It has water, a breathable atmosphere, and diverse ecosystems.

  • Earth is about 4.54 billion years old.
  • It is the fifth largest planet in the solar system.
  • Earth’s atmosphere is composed mainly of nitrogen and oxygen.
  • About 71% of Earth’s surface is covered with water.
  • It has one natural satellite: the Moon.
  • Earth rotates on its axis once every 24 hours, causing day and night.
  • It takes about 365.25 days for Earth to orbit the Sun.
  • Earth has a layered structure: crust, mantle, outer core, and inner core.
  • Plate tectonics on Earth cause earthquakes and volcanic activity.
  • Earth's magnetic field protects it from solar radiation.
